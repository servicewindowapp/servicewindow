# ServiceWindow — Competitive Intelligence
> Last updated: 2026-04-18

---

## Orderup Apps
**URL**: orderupapps.net | **Status**: Monitor — not a direct competitor today

### What They Are
Custom mobile app builder for individual food truck operators. Builds branded iOS/Android apps for a single truck — customers use it to place orders, earn loyalty points, track GPS, get push notifications.

### Pricing
- $899 one-time development fee
- $99.99/month subscription
- No transaction fees

### Features
- Custom branded ordering app (iOS + Android)
- Square/Clover POS integration
- GPS location tracking
- Loyalty points + coupon system
- Push notifications + SMS
- IVR phone system with included number
- DoorDash/Uber Eats integration (no platform fees)
- Kitchen Display System

### Second Product: Orderup Customers (orderupcustomers.com)
Catering event management software. Helps individual trucks organize their own catering bookings. Not a marketplace — no demand side.

### Why They Joined the SWFL Facebook Group (Apr 17, 2026)
Prospecting for truck operators to sell $99.99/mo subscriptions. Same supply pool ServiceWindow needs.

### Head-to-Head

| Factor | Orderup Apps | ServiceWindow |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| Product type | App for truck's existing customers | Marketplace connecting trucks to new demand |
| Demand side | None | HOA managers, venues, planners |
| Verification | None | Core platform feature |
| Price to truck | $99.99/mo + $899 setup | $29-49/mo, no setup fee, 30-day free trial |
| SWFL focus | Generic national product | SWFL-only, geo-locked |
| HOA/venue segment | Not mentioned | Primary demand segment |

### Real Risk
Not direct competition. Budget competition — a truck paying $99.99/mo may feel the marketing problem is "solved." Bigger risk: if they add a marketplace layer, they have existing truck relationships. Watch for product announcements.

### Counter-Pitch (if truck already has Orderup)
"Orderup handles your repeat customers. ServiceWindow gets you in front of HOA managers booking 2nd Saturdays year-round, beer gardens, and event planners who can't find trucks on Facebook. Different tool, different revenue stream."

## Roaming Hunger
**URL**: roaminghunger.com | **Status**: National competitor, weak on ServiceWindow's strengths

National food truck booking marketplace. No SWFL-specific focus. No verification of operators. No HOA/venue relationship features. Serves primarily large corporate events. Their weakness is ServiceWindow's core proposition — local, verified, relationship-focused.

---

## Facebook Groups (Primary Incumbent)
**Status**: The platform everyone is currently using by default

- Food Truck Connection of SWFL — 3,500 members
- SWFL Food Truck Depot — 34,900 members
- Collier & Lee County Food Trucks — smaller

Weaknesses that ServiceWindow directly exploits:
- ~6 scam posts per day blocked in the largest group
- No confirmation system — 44 comments respond to one HOA post, no resolution
- No filtering by cuisine, geography, or capacity
- No recurring booking mechanism
- Out-of-area noise (West Palm Beach posts in SWFL groups)
- Last-minute cancellations with no recovery path

---

## What to Watch
- Orderup Apps product announcements — any marketplace or booking feature = escalate threat level
- New entrants joining any of the 3 SWFL Facebook groups who look like tech companies
