# Market Research Summary — SWFL Food Truck Market
> Synthesized from daily scrapes: 2026-03-29 through 2026-04-17

---

## The Problem We're Solving
Food truck operators and demand-side users (HOA managers, venue owners, event planners) currently use Facebook groups as their primary marketplace:
- **Food Truck Connection of SWFL** — 3,500 members (booking requests only)
- **SWFL Food Truck Depot** — 34,900 members (highest volume, most noise)
- **Collier & Lee County Food Trucks** — lower activity, Naples/Collier focus

**Core pain points:**
- ~6 scam posts per day blocked in the largest group
- Out-of-area posts pollute the feed (West Palm Beach requests in SWFL groups)
- No confirmation system — trucks respond in comments, no accountability
- Last-minute cancellations with no recovery path
- No cuisine filtering (can't request "1 food truck + 1 dessert truck" in a structured way)
- High comment volume on good posts (44 comments on one HOA request) = chaos

## Recurring Pain Points to Build Against
1. **Scams** — explicit warnings in every high-volume post; verification badge = platform's #1 wedge
2. **Last-minute cancellations** — Easter egg hunt truck no-show 3 days out; Southerly at Heron Creek same-day
3. **No filtering** — can't specify cuisine type, service area, or capacity in FB posts
4. **Geographic noise** — West Palm Beach posts in SWFL groups = platform geo-lock is a feature
5. **No confirmation** — 44 comments respond to one HOA post; no way to know who got the slot

## Phase 2 Feature Signals From Research
- SMS alerts for urgent same-day slots (Earth Day scenario)
- Standby/waitlist when truck cancels (Easter egg hunt scenario)
- Recurring booking templates (Tiffany Allard books 2nd Saturdays all year)
- Multi-truck request (Tiffany wanted 1 food truck + 1 dessert truck simultaneously)
- Permanent spot listings (Mark Kasper asking about Naples permanent space)
