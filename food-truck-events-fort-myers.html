Planning a Food Truck Event in Fort Myers — What to Know

Southwest Florida's event calendar is driven by its seasonal population. The region swells from November through April as seasonal residents and tourists arrive, and events during this window are heavily attended. If you're planning a public food truck event during peak season, expect strong turnout and book your truck lineup well in advance — popular operators fill up fast.

Summer events are absolutely viable, especially in the evening. Many of Fort Myers' best food truck nights happen after 6pm when temperatures drop and outdoor dining becomes comfortable again. Shaded venues and covered spaces make daytime summer events workable for a dedicated crowd.

For recurring events — a monthly truck night, a weekly lunch program, a seasonal festival — ServiceWindow's Shift Marketplace lets operators post recurring availability, making it easier to establish ongoing relationships with trucks rather than renegotiating every cycle.

Lead time matters. For weekend events during season, aim for 4 to 6 weeks minimum. For weekday events or smaller gatherings in the off-season, 2 weeks is usually sufficient. The earlier you post, the more offers you receive.
